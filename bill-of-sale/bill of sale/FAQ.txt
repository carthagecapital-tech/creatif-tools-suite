GETTING STARTED
===============

Q: How do I open the file?
A: Double-click billofsale-pro.html. It opens in your default browser.
   If it opens in a text editor instead, right-click the file, choose
   "Open With", and pick Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge.

Q: Do I need to install anything?
A: No. The app is a single HTML file. It runs entirely in your browser.
   No installation, no plugins, no extensions.

Q: Do I need an internet connection?
A: Only for the first download. After saving billofsale-pro.html to your
   device, you can use it offline forever.

Q: Does this work on my phone or tablet?
A: Yes. The app is fully responsive and works on iPhone, iPad, and
   Android devices. Open the file in your mobile browser (Safari on
   iOS, Chrome on Android).

Q: Does this work on Mac / Windows / Linux / Chromebook?
A: Yes to all. Any modern operating system with a modern browser works.


LEGAL QUESTIONS
===============

Q: Is this a legal document?
A: The bill of sale produced by this app is a printable form that you
   can use for private vehicle sales. It includes federal odometer
   disclosure and state-specific disclosures for 12 US states. However:

   - It is NOT legal advice.
   - It is NOT a substitute for an attorney.
   - It may not satisfy every requirement for unusual transactions
     (lien sales, estate sales with multiple heirs, sales involving
     trusts, sales to out-of-state buyers, etc.).

   For unusual situations, consult a licensed attorney in your state.

Q: Do I need to notarize the bill of sale?
A: Three US states REQUIRE notarization for vehicle bill of sale:

     - Florida
     - Pennsylvania
     - North Carolina

   For other states, notarization is optional. Some buyers or sellers
   choose to notarize voluntarily for extra legal protection.

Q: Is this valid for out-of-state sales?
A: The bill of sale is generated based on the state YOU select in the
   app. If the buyer is from a different state, check both states'
   requirements. Most states accept a bill of sale from another state
   as long as it includes the basic transaction details, but verify
   with both DMVs to be safe.

Q: What about emissions / smog requirements?
A: BillOfSale Pro does NOT generate a smog certificate or emissions test
   result. These are separate documents handled by licensed test
   stations. Some states require them at the time of sale - the
   relevant state's disclosure (if pre-filled) will mention this.

Q: What about liens on the vehicle?
A: If the vehicle has an outstanding lien (loan), the lien must be
   satisfied BEFORE the sale. The buyer needs a clean title. Some
   states require an additional "lien release" document from the
   lienholder. BillOfSale Pro has a "lienholder" field in the Car
   vehicle type for record-keeping, but does not generate the lien
   release document itself.

Q: What if I'm gifting the vehicle to a family member?
A: Use the "Gift transfer" toggle in Section 04 (Sale). The bill of
   sale will show "$0.00 - Gift" as the price. Some states require
   additional paperwork for gift transfers (to document that no sales
   tax is owed). Check your state DMV's gift transfer requirements.

Q: What if there's a trade-in?
A: Use the "Trade-in involved" toggle in Section 04 (Sale). A sub-form
   appears for the trade-in vehicle (VIN, description, value). The
   printed bill of sale shows both vehicles and the net sale price.

Q: What about vehicles over 10 years old?
A: The federal odometer disclosure requirement applies to vehicles
   under 10 model years old AND under 16,000 lbs GVWR. For older
   vehicles, the odometer disclosure is optional but still
   recommended. The app includes the field either way.

Q: What about vehicles sold for parts only / not operational?
A: BillOfSale Pro is designed for vehicles being sold "as is" for
   normal use. For parts-only sales or non-operational vehicles,
   consider having an attorney draft a specific bill of sale that
   includes the parts-only disclaimer.


TECHNICAL QUESTIONS
===================

Q: Why is the file one big HTML file? Can I split it?
A: It's a single file by design. This makes it portable (email it,
   drop it on a USB stick, store it anywhere) and ensures it works
   offline. Splitting the file would break the portability.

Q: Why doesn't it have a download button / fancy installer?
A: Same reason - single file = portable, offline, simple. Many
   buyers prefer a file they own and control over a subscription
   or web app.

Q: Can I customize the design / add my branding?
A: The HTML and CSS are editable. If you're comfortable with HTML,
   you can open billofsale-pro.html in any text editor and modify
   colors, fonts, or layout. The navy color is set via CSS variables
   near the top of the file. Be careful - changes may break the
   print layout.

Q: Can I add my own state disclosures?
A: Yes, if you're comfortable with JavaScript. Open the file in a
   text editor, find the STATE_DISCLOSURES section (search for that
   word), and add your state following the same pattern as the
   existing 12 states.

Q: Can I save data to a server instead of just localStorage?
A: Not without modifying the source code. The app is intentionally
   offline-only for privacy reasons. If you need server-side
   storage, consider a different product.

Q: Why does it use localStorage instead of files on my disk?
A: Browser security model. JavaScript in a webpage cannot write files
   directly to your disk without a download dialog. localStorage is
   the standard way to persist data in a web app. Your data stays
   on your device, in your browser's storage for this file.

Q: How do I backup my drafts?
A: BillOfSale Pro stores drafts in localStorage, which is bound to
   your browser and device. To back up:

     1. Use the app, fill in a draft
     2. Print to PDF (Ctrl/Cmd + P > Save as PDF) - this gives you
        a permanent copy of that draft as a bill of sale document
     3. Save the PDF somewhere safe (cloud drive, USB stick, etc.)

   The PDF is the actual bill of sale, not the editable form data.
   If you need to come back and edit the draft later, you can either
   re-fill the form or use the in-app drafts (if you're still on the
   same browser/device).

Q: Does it work in Chrome / Firefox / Safari / Edge?
A: Yes to all four. Tested on the latest versions of each. Internet
   Explorer is NOT supported (it's been retired by Microsoft).

Q: Does it work on mobile browsers?
A: Yes. The layout adapts to phone and tablet screens. On small
   screens, the form appears above the preview with a floating
   "Preview" button to jump to the document.

Q: How big is the file? Will it load fast?
A: The HTML file is approximately 100-120 KB. It loads instantly
   on any device with any modern browser. No large assets, no
   external scripts, no CDN calls.


PRINTING AND PDF
================

Q: How do I save as PDF?
A: Open the print dialog (Ctrl/Cmd + P or click "Print / PDF" in
   the top bar), then:
     - Chrome: change Destination to "Save as PDF"
     - Edge: change Printer to "Microsoft Print to PDF"
     - Firefox: change Printer to "Microsoft Print to PDF" or use
       the "Save" option in the print dialog
     - Safari: click the PDF dropdown at the bottom of the print
       dialog, choose "Save as PDF"

Q: The PDF has weird margins / doesn't fit on one page
A: Make sure your browser's print scale is set to 100% (not "Fit
   to page" or "Shrink to fit"). The bill of sale is already sized
   for US Letter paper.

Q: Can I print on A4 paper (Europe / international)?
A: Yes. In the print dialog, change the paper size to A4. The
   document layout will reflow automatically.

Q: Why does the print have a copyright footer on every page?
A: This is intentional. Legal documents often include provenance
   information (when and by whom it was generated). The footer
   also includes page numbers, which is standard for multi-page
   documents.

Q: Can I remove the footer from the printed document?
A: Not without modifying the source code. The footer is in the
   @media print CSS block at the top of billofsale-pro.html.

STATE COVERAGE
==============

Q: Why does my state not have pre-filled disclosures?
A: Twelve US states have full pre-filled disclosure data: CA, NY,
   FL, TX, IL, OH, PA, WA, GA, NC, AZ, MI. These were chosen
   because they represent the highest-volume states and the states
   with the most distinctive bill-of-sale requirements.

   For other states, the app shows a verification notice reminding
   you to check your state DMV. The basic bill of sale form is
   still produced - it just doesn't include state-specific
   clauses.

Q: Can you add my state?
A: Message me on Etsy with your state and any specific requirements
   you'd like included. I add states based on buyer requests.

Q: Does this work for the District of Columbia?
A: Yes. DC is included in the state selector.
