§ 06help & reference

§ 01How to Use

The Handmade Pricing Calculator helps you price your handmade goods fairly and profitably.

  • Enter your costs — Material cost, labor hours, overhead, and packaging for each item.
  • Set your target — Your desired hourly wage and target profit margin.
  • Choose your channel — Different platforms charge different fees (Etsy, Shopify, Craft Fair, Wholesale).
  • Enter your selling price — See your true hourly wage and whether you're meeting your target.
  • Save products — Build a catalog for tracking and analysis.

Important: All your data is stored only on your computer. Nothing is sent to the internet. Back up regularly using the Export feature.

§ 05Pricing Strategy Guide

Know your true costs. Many makers underprice because they forget overhead, packaging, and platform fees. Include everything.

Price for profit, not just materials. Your time is valuable. Charge for every hour you spend making, sourcing, and finishing.

Account for channel fees. Etsy takes ~9.5% + fees. Shopify is ~2.9% + $0.30. Build these into your price.

Use batch sizing. If materials produce multiple units, enter the full material cost and set batch size to divide it properly.

Wholesale math: Wholesale buyers typically pay 30-50% of retail. Use the multiplier slider and ensure you still cover costs.

Craft fair reality: Divide your booth fee by realistic sales volume. A $100 booth with 20 expected sales = $5 per unit.
