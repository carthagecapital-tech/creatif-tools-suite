  HANDMADE PRICING CALCULATOR
  Quick Start Guide (5 minutes)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This guide will walk you through pricing
your first product.

STEP 1 — OPEN THE APP
─────────────────────
Double‑click the HTML file. The calculator
view opens automatically.

STEP 2 — ENTER YOUR COSTS
─────────────────────────
Fill in these fields:

• Product Name  → “Silver Ring”
• Material Cost → how much the raw
                   materials cost
• Batch Size    → how many items you can
                   make from one batch of
                   materials (leave at 1
                   if unsure)
• Labor Hours   → how long it takes to
                   make one item
• Hourly Wage   → what you want to earn
                   per hour (e.g., $20)
• Overhead      → ongoing costs per item
                   (tools, studio rent,
                    electricity)
• Packaging     → cost of box, wrapping,
                   labels

STEP 3 — CHOOSE YOUR CHANNEL
────────────────────────────
Use the “Selling Channel” dropdown to pick
where you sell:

• Etsy      → includes Etsy's fees
• Shopify   → includes payment processing
• Craft Fair → add your booth fee
• Wholesale  → price for bulk buyers

STEP 4 — SET YOUR SELLING PRICE
───────────────────────────────
Enter the price you plan to charge in the
“Your Selling Price” field. The cards on
screen instantly show:

• Suggested Price    → what the formula
                       recommends
• True Hourly Wage   → what you actually
                       earn per hour
• Profit Margin      → your markup
• Break‑Even Price   → the absolute
                       minimum you can
                       charge

STEP 5 — SAVE YOUR PRODUCT
──────────────────────────
Click the “Save Product” button (or press
Ctrl+S on your keyboard). The product is
added to your catalog.

STEP 6 — EXPLORE
────────────────
Use the sidebar to switch between views:
• Calculator  → price new items
• Products    → see all saved products
• Compare     → test two scenarios
                side‑by‑side
• Analytics   → charts showing your
                average wage and margins
• Settings    → change default wage,
                overhead, color theme
• Help        → full formula reference
                and keyboard shortcuts

YOU'RE DONE!
────────────────
You've priced your first handmade item
fairly and profitably. Repeat for every
product in your shop.

Next step: Open the User Guide for deep
dives into batch pricing, wholesale
strategies, and backing up your data.
