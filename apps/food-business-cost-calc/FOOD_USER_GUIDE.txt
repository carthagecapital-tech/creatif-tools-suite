1. OVERVIEW
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Food Business Cost Calculator is a browser-based tool
for cottage bakers, food market vendors, and small food
business owners who need accurate per-unit costs and
pricing for every recipe they sell.

It accounts for every real cost — ingredients, yield
loss, labor, packaging, overhead, booth fees, and
mileage — so you know exactly what to charge to cover
your costs and pay yourself fairly.

All data is stored locally in your browser. Nothing is
uploaded anywhere.

Navigate using the sidebar on the left. On mobile, use
the menu button (☰) to open it.

2. RECIPE CALCULATOR — TAB 01
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RECIPE DETAILS
──────────────
  Recipe Name     — Label for saving and tracking.

  Category        — Baked Goods, Jams & Preserves,
                    Sauces & Condiments, Bread,
                    Candy & Confections, Frozen Treats,
                    Meal Kits, or Other.

  Batch Yield     — How many units this recipe produces
                    (e.g., "this recipe makes 12 cookies").

  I Want to Make  — Scale the batch up or down. All
                    ingredient quantities and costs
                    rescale automatically.

INGREDIENTS
───────────
Click "Add Ingredient" for each ingredient. For each:

  Ingredient Name   — Free text label.
  Purchase Qty      — How much you bought (e.g., 500g).
  Purchase Unit     — The unit (g, kg, oz, lb, ml, l,
                      tsp, tbsp, cup, or unit).
  Purchase Price    — What you paid for that quantity.
  Recipe Qty Used   — How much this recipe requires.
  Recipe Unit       — Must be in the same unit group as
                      the purchase unit (weight, volume,
                      or count). Cross-group conversions
                      are flagged as warnings.
  Yield Loss %      — Percentage lost to waste (peeling,
                      trimming, cooking loss). See the
                      Yield Loss Reference section below.

COSTS & OVERHEADS
─────────────────
  Hours to Make       — Time to produce one batch.
  Hourly Rate         — What you want to pay yourself
                        per hour worked.
  Packaging per Unit  — Cost of boxes, labels, bags, etc.
  Kitchen Overhead    — Per-batch share of utilities,
                        equipment, and running costs.
  Booth Fee           — Market stall fee for the event.
  Units Expected      — How many units you plan to sell
                        at the event. The booth fee is
                        divided across these units.
  Round Trip Miles    — Distance to market. The IRS
                        mileage rate is applied to
                        calculate a travel cost per batch.

TARGET MARGIN
─────────────
A slider from 10% to 80%. Adjusting it recalculates
your suggested selling price in real time. A 40% margin
is a recommended starting point for cottage food sellers.

RESULTS
───────
  Ingredient Cost   — Total raw ingredient cost for batch
  Packaging Cost    — Total packaging for the batch
  Labor Cost        — Hours x hourly rate
  Overhead & Extras — Kitchen overhead + mileage + booth
  Total Batch Cost  — Sum of all costs
  Cost Per Unit     — Total batch cost / units produced
  Suggested Price   — Price needed to hit target margin
  Net Profit        — Revenue minus total batch cost
  Profit Per Unit   — Net profit divided by units
  Margin %          — Net profit as % of revenue
  True Hourly Wage  — Net profit divided by labor hours
  Break-Even Price  — Minimum price to cover all costs

ACTIONS
───────
  Save Recipe      — Stores the recipe to your catalog
  Reset Form       — Clears all inputs
  Send to Compare  — Sends to Compare tab (Side A or B)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7. YIELD LOSS REFERENCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Yield loss accounts for waste when preparing ingredients.
Enter the percentage lost for each ingredient.

  Onions          20%   (peeling, trimming)
  Butter          10%   (pan residue)
  Carrots         15%   (peeling, tops)
  Potatoes        18%   (peeling, eyes)
  Apples          15%   (coring, peeling)
  Citrus          25%   (zest only, juicing)
  Fresh herbs     30%   (stems, bruised leaves)
  Chocolate        5%   (melting residue)
  Flour (sifted)   5%   (sifting loss)
  Meat (bone-in) 25-35% (bone, trim)
  Coffee beans    15%   (chaff, grind loss)
  Nuts (shelled)   5%   (shell fragments, dust)

Leave yield loss at 0% for dry goods and pre-measured
ingredients where there is no meaningful waste.

8. UNIT CONVERSION REFERENCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Units within the same group convert automatically.
Cross-group conversions (e.g., grams to ml) are not
supported and will show a warning on that ingredient.

  Weight group:   g, kg, oz, lb
    1 kg = 1000 g | 1 lb = 16 oz

  Volume group:   ml, l, tsp, tbsp, cup
    1 l = 1000 ml | 1 tbsp = 3 tsp
    1 cup = 16 tbsp = 48 tsp

  Count group:    unit
    No conversion needed

9. PROFITABILITY BADGE GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Each saved recipe receives a badge based on performance.
Badges are evaluated in order — first match wins.

  Underpriced   — True hourly wage is below 80% of
                  your target hourly rate
  Low Margin    — Margin is under 20%
  Healthy       — Margin is 20% to 40%
  Strong        — Margin is 40% or above

True Hourly Wage color coding:
  Green   — At or above your target hourly rate
  Amber   — Within 80% of target but below it
  Red     — Below 80% of your target rate

10. FORMULA REFERENCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Scaling Factor      = Desired Batch / Batch Yield
  Scaled Recipe Qty   = Recipe Qty Used x Scaling Factor
  Effective Qty       = Scaled Recipe Qty / (1 - Yield Loss% / 100)
  Unit Cost           = Purchase Price / Purchase Qty
  Ingredient Cost     = Effective Qty x Unit Cost

  Total Packaging     = Packaging Per Unit x Desired Batch
  Total Labor         = Labor Hours x Hourly Rate
  Mileage Cost        = Round Trip Miles x IRS Mileage Rate
  Booth Allocation    = (Booth Fee / Event Units) x Desired Batch
  Overhead & Extras   = Overhead + Mileage Cost + Booth Allocation
  Total Batch Cost    = Ingredients + Packaging + Labor + Overhead & Extras

  Cost Per Unit       = Total Batch Cost / Desired Batch
  Suggested Price     = Cost Per Unit / (1 - Target Margin% / 100)
  Net Profit          = (Suggested Price x Desired Batch) - Total Batch Cost
  Profit Per Unit     = Net Profit / Desired Batch
  Margin %            = (Net Profit / Projected Revenue) x 100
  True Hourly Wage    = Net Profit / Labor Hours
  Break-Even Price    = Total Batch Cost / Desired Batch

11. PRICING STRATEGY GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Know your true costs
  Factor in every ingredient, your time, packaging,
  overhead, and market fees. The true hourly wage result
  tells you whether you're actually paying yourself fairly.

Start at 40% margin
  A 40% target margin is a solid starting point for
  cottage food sellers. Specialty or gourmet items can
  often command 50-60%.

Account for booth fees
  If your market charges $40 and you sell 50 units, each
  unit carries an $0.80 booth cost. Enter the booth fee
  and expected units to see the real per-unit impact.

Don't forget mileage
  At the IRS rate (~$0.67/mi), a 20-mile round trip costs
  $13.40 per market day. That comes directly out of profit.

Use price psychology
  Round suggested prices up to whole-dollar amounts
  ($8.00 instead of $7.83). Customers perceive quality
  at clean price points.

Test batch scaling
  Use the Compare tab to see how doubling your batch
  affects cost per unit and hourly wage. Bigger batches
  usually improve margins significantly.

Track everything
  Save every recipe iteration. Use the Analytics tab
  to identify which recipes earn the most per hour —
  then focus on those.
