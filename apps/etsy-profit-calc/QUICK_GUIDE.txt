EtsyCalc Pro – Quick Start Guide
═══════════════════════════════════

1. SET YOUR MARKET
   On the Calculator tab, choose your country from the dropdown.
   This selects the correct currency and Etsy fee rates.

2. FILL IN THE DETAILS
   - Product name (anything you like, for your reference)
   - Selling price (the price you list on Etsy)
   - Shipping cost (what you pay to ship it)
   - Material cost (cost of raw materials per item)
   - Packaging cost (boxes, labels, etc.)

3. TURN ON ETSY ADS (if you use them)
   Flip the toggle switch on. The app automatically applies the
   15% offsite ad fee (adjustable if you have a lower rate).

4. SET YOUR TARGET PROFIT MARGIN
   Use the slider to choose a goal (default 40%). The calculator
   will suggest a price that achieves that margin after all fees.

5. READ YOUR RESULTS
   - Fee breakdown shows listing, transaction, and payment fees.
   - Profit analysis shows net profit, margin, and total costs.
   - A coloured fee bar visualises where every dollar goes.

6. SAVE THE LISTING
   Click the "save listing" button. It stores the calculation so
   you can compare later.

7. VIEW AND COMPARE SAVED LISTINGS
   Switch to the Saved Listings tab. Here you can:
   - See all your saved products
   - Sort by name, price, profit, or margin
   - Filter profitable vs. loss-making items
   - Export everything to a CSV file (works in Excel, Sheets)
   - Import a previously saved CSV file
   - Edit or delete any listing

8. CHECK ANALYTICS
   The Analytics tab shows pie and bar charts for your most
   recent saved listing. At a glance, see where the money goes.

9. SET DEFAULTS
   In Settings, you can set default shipping, packaging, and
   material costs. Next time you start a new calculation, those
   fields will be pre-filled for you.

10. KEYBOARD SHORTCUTS
    Ctrl/Cmd + S  – Save current listing
    Ctrl/Cmd + E  – Export all listings as CSV
    Ctrl/Cmd + P  – Print the current view
    Ctrl/Cmd + K  – Focus the search box
    Esc           – Close any open dialog

───
That’s it! You’ll be making informed pricing decisions in minutes.
