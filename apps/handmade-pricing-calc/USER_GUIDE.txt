1. WHAT THE APP DOES
────────────────────────────────────────
This tool helps handmade sellers (Etsy,
Shopify, craft fairs, wholesale) calculate
a profitable selling price.

It accounts for:
• Materials & batch sizing
• Your hourly labor rate
• Overhead and packaging costs
• Platform transaction fees
• Profit margin targets
• Wholesale pricing multipliers
• Craft fair booth fees

All calculations are done entirely on your
computer. No data ever leaves your device.

────────────────────────────────────────
2. THE CALCULATOR — FIELD BY FIELD
────────────────────────────────────────

PRODUCT NAME
  Give your item a descriptive name
  (e.g., “Silver Moon Pendant”).

MATERIAL COST ($)
  The total cost of raw materials for ONE
  batch (see BATCH SIZE).

BATCH SIZE
  How many items you can produce from the
  materials entered above. Example:
  You buy $30 of clay that makes 10
  pendants → Material Cost = $30,
  Batch Size = 10 → cost per item = $3.

LABOR HOURS
  Time to make one item. Use decimals:
  0.5 = 30 minutes, 1.25 = 1 hour 15 min.

HOURLY WAGE ($/HR)
  What you want to pay yourself per hour.
  The default ($20) comes from Settings.

OVERHEAD ($)
  Indirect costs per item: studio rent,
  electricity, equipment wear, website
  hosting. Default comes from Settings.

PACKAGING ($)
  Cost of gift box, tissue paper, labels,
  shipping mailer per item.

SELLING CHANNEL
  Where you sell. See Section 4 for fee
  details.

BOOTH FEE ($) — Craft Fair only
  The total fee you paid for your fair
  booth. Spread across expected units.

EXPECTED UNITS SOLD — Craft Fair only
  Realistic number of items you'll sell
  at that fair. The booth fee is divided
  by this number.

WHOLESALE MULTIPLIER — Wholesale only
  Wholesale buyers typically pay 30‑50%
  of retail. Adjust the slider to see
  what your wholesale price would be.

TARGET MARGIN %
  The profit percentage you want above
  your total costs. Default 50%.

YOUR SELLING PRICE ($)
  The price you intend to charge. All
  result cards update instantly as you
  type.

────────────────────────────────────────
3. UNDERSTANDING THE RESULT CARDS
────────────────────────────────────────

SUGGESTED PRICE
  The price that hits your target margin
  after covering all costs and channel
  fees.

TRUE HOURLY WAGE
  Your actual hourly earnings at the
  selling price you entered. The card
  border is color‑coded:
    Green  → meeting or exceeding your
             target wage
    Yellow → close to target
    Red    → underpaying yourself

PROFIT MARGIN
  Percentage of selling price that is
  pure profit after all costs.

TOTAL COST / UNIT
  Sum of materials, labor, overhead,
  packaging, and booth costs per item.

MARKUP
  How much above total cost you're
  charging, as a percentage.

BREAK‑EVEN PRICE
  The lowest possible price — covers
  every cost with $0 profit. Never sell
  below this number.

WHOLESALE PRICE (only in Wholesale mode)
  The price a bulk buyer would pay,
  calculated from the suggested price
  using your wholesale multiplier.

────────────────────────────────────────
4. SELLING CHANNELS & FEES
────────────────────────────────────────

ETSY
  Transaction fee:   6.5%
  Payment processing: 3% + $0.25
  Listing fee:        $0.20 per item

SHOPIFY
  Transaction fee:   0% (unless using
                      Shopify Payments)
  Payment processing: 2.9% + $0.30

CRAFT FAIR
  No platform fees. You enter your booth
  fee and expected units sold. The app
  spreads the booth cost per item.

WHOLESALE
  No platform fees. The wholesale
  multiplier slider determines the
  discounted price.

────────────────────────────────────────
5. SAVING & MANAGING PRODUCTS
────────────────────────────────────────

SAVE A PRODUCT
  Click “Save Product” (or Ctrl+S). The
  product appears in your catalog.

LOAD A PRODUCT
  In the Products table, click “Load”
  next to any item. It fills the
  calculator so you can adjust and
  re‑save.

DUPLICATE A PRODUCT
  Click “Dup” to make an editable copy.

DELETE A PRODUCT
  Click “Del” → a confirmation dialog
  appears. You can also select multiple
  products with the checkboxes and click
  “Delete Selected”.

UNSAVED CHANGES
  When you modify the calculator, a dot
  (●) appears on the Save button. If you
  try to close the page, the browser will
  ask if you want to leave without saving.

────────────────────────────────────────
6. PRODUCT TABLE — SORT, FILTER, SEARCH
────────────────────────────────────────

SORT
  Click any column header to sort the
  table. Click again to reverse.

FILTER
  Use the chip buttons to show only
  products from a specific channel or
  with a certain health status (Strong,
  Fair, Underpriced).

SEARCH
  Type in the sidebar search box to find
  products by name. The list filters as
  you type.

PAGINATION
  When you have more than 10 products,
  page controls appear at the bottom.

────────────────────────────────────────
7. COMPARE VIEW
────────────────────────────────────────

Test two different pricing scenarios side
by side. Enter values in Column A and
Column B, then click “Compare Scenarios”.

Results show:
• Suggested price
• True hourly wage
• Profit margin
• Total cost
• Break‑even price

The winner (higher hourly wage) is
highlighted.

Use the Reset Both button to start over.

────────────────────────────────────────
8. ANALYTICS VIEW
────────────────────────────────────────

Shows summary statistics for all saved
products:

• Average profit margin
• Average hourly wage
• Total number of products
• Counts by health: Strong, Fair,
  Underpriced

Charts:
• Bar chart — top 10 products by true
  hourly wage
• Pie chart — average cost breakdown
  (materials, labor, overhead, packaging,
   fees)

Charts include hidden data tables for
screen reader users.

────────────────────────────────────────
9. SETTINGS
────────────────────────────────────────

DEFAULT VALUES
  Set your preferred hourly wage, overhead,
  packaging cost, channel, and booth fee.
  These become the defaults for new
  products.

APPEARANCE
  Change the accent color hue (0‑360) to
  personalize the look. The preview
  updates as you slide.

DATA MANAGEMENT
  • Export JSON — save all products and
    settings as a backup file
  • Import JSON — restore from a backup
    (you'll be asked to confirm before
    overwriting)
  • Clear All Data — permanently delete
    everything

────────────────────────────────────────
10. IMPORT & EXPORT (BACKUPS)
────────────────────────────────────────

EXPORT JSON (Settings)
  Downloads a .json file containing all
  your products and settings. Store this
  somewhere safe (cloud drive, USB stick).

IMPORT JSON (Settings)
  Select your backup file and click Import.
  If you already have products saved, you
  will be asked to confirm — importing
  REPLACES your current catalog.

EXPORT CSV (Calculator or Products view)
  Downloads a .csv file that opens in
  Excel, Google Sheets, or Numbers. Useful
  for accounting or sharing with an
  accountant.

Back up your data regularly!

12. TROUBLESHOOTING
────────────────────────────────────────

Q: The app looks wrong — dark background
   but light panels.
A: Click the ☾/☀ button in the sidebar to
   toggle dark/light mode. If that doesn't
   fix it, try clicking it twice.

Q: I typed a price but nothing changed.
A: All fields update automatically. Check
   that you've entered a number in every
   field.

Q: My data disappeared!
A: If you cleared your browser history or
   cookies, the app's data may be gone.
   Always keep a JSON backup. If you used
   a private/incognito window, data is not
   saved between sessions.

Q: The app won't open.
A: Make sure you're double‑clicking the
   .html file, not trying to open it in a
   text editor. Right‑click → Open With →
   your browser.

Q: Charts are blurry.
A: Resize the browser window slightly, or
   navigate away from Analytics and back.

────────────────────────────────────────
13. DATA PRIVACY & SAFETY
────────────────────────────────────────

This app runs entirely on your computer.
No data is sent to the internet. No
analytics, no tracking, no third‑party
services.

All information is stored in your
browser's localStorage, which is like a
tiny private database only your browser
can access.

To protect your data:
• Export a JSON backup regularly
• Don't use incognito mode if you want
  data to persist
• Keep your browser updated

If you sell your computer, clear your
browser data first — or just delete the
backup file.
