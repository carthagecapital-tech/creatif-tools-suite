Why these apps

Most home-based and small food businesses underprice their menus. They account for ingredients but forget packaging, labor, overhead, and waste. These calculators force you to think about every cost line so your menu prices reflect reality — and your business stays profitable.

How to choose the right app

  • Start with one signature recipe. Calculate its true cost (ingredients + packaging + labor), then set a menu price with 60-70% gross margin.
  • Update your ingredient costs quarterly — supplier prices change and your calculations need to follow.
  • Use the batch scaler to test whether making larger batches actually saves money (it often doesn't for labor-heavy recipes).

Frequently asked questions

What kinds of food businesses is this for?
Bakeries, food trucks, cottage food businesses, meal prep services, small restaurants, and home-based food sellers. If you sell food and need to know your cost per item, this is for you.
Can I save multiple recipes?
Yes. The recipe cost calculator lets you build a library of recipes with full cost breakdowns.
