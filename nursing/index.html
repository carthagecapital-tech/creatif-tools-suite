Why these apps

Hospital EHR systems are powerful but slow. These apps work alongside them — use them to capture data quickly during your shift, then transcribe to the EHR. Or use them as your primary charting tool if you're in a non-EHR setting. Either way, no subscription, no cloud sync, no PHI exposure.

How to choose the right app

  • For ER: track patient status, room, and acuity in real-time. The SBAR handoff generator saves minutes at shift change.
  • For ICU: the vent/CRRT/vasoactive drip tracker with hourly auto-totals replaces the paper flow sheet.
  • For Med-Surg: the 5-step shift report with auto-summary is a game-changer for end-of-shift handoff.

Frequently asked questions

Is this HIPAA compliant?
The apps are local-first, so no PHI leaves your device. This puts them outside the scope of HIPAA (which regulates covered entities and their business associates, not local file storage). That said, check with your facility's compliance team before using any third-party tool at work.
Can I import from my hospital's EHR?
Not directly. The apps are designed to work standalone, capturing data quickly during your shift. You can then transfer key information to your EHR.
